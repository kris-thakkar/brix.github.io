The smart money move: channel strategy that protects working capital.

If retail margin > 10% → BRIX takes dealership. If < 10% → BRIX rents the space to a dealer (concession), saving warehouse space + working capital, earning stable rent.

The risks—said straight.

  • This becomes a showroom, not a supply chain. If contractors don’t switch, demand stays shallow.
  • Growth hides losses. Discount-led GMV destroys EBITDA.
  • Quick-commerce can quietly go negative. Heavy items can turn delivery into a loss engine.
  • Inventory becomes a cash prison. Slow movers lock working capital.
  • Stockouts kill trust. Construction customers don’t forgive unreliability.
  • Incumbents fight with relationships. Supplier pressure and temporary price wars are real.
  • Execution is the moat—and the risk. Many SKUs require perfect ops discipline.
  • Working capital kills more businesses than competition. Cash cycle management must be non-negotiable.

Key de-risking design principle: Own only profitable categories; convert low-margin bulk into rent-backed partner inventory.

If things go south: how fast can we liquidate and cap losses?

Our downside is capped by design: concessions reduce inventory risk, assets are recoverable, and lease terms are structured to allow transfer or break.

0–30 days
  • Liquidate fast-moving inventory via bulk dealer/distributor sales + clearance
  • Freeze credit; collect receivables fast
  • Concession rent acts as temporary cash buffer
30–90 days
  • Sell racking/MHE and warehouse assets (high recoverability if standard)
  • Sell fleet (liquid secondary market)
90–180 days
  • Transfer/sublease lease using pre-negotiated rights and break clauses
  • Fitout is partially sunk; use modular fitout to reduce downside

Better than liquidation: strategic sale

Sell the package to large dealers/distributors, organized B2B construction commerce players, developer procurement arms, or logistics/fulfillment players.

Package = lease + warehouse setup + fleet + trained ops + contractor base + vendor network.

Investor takeaway line: Downside is capped by design; concessions reduce inventory risk, assets are recoverable, and leases are structured for transfer/break.

Investor FAQ (fast clarity)

How does quick commerce work for heavy materials?

Heavy/bulky SKUs go to scheduled delivery lanes with minimum order rules. Light/medium SKUs stay in the fast lane for SLA wins.

Why are concessions a feature, not a compromise?

They convert low-margin bulk into predictable rent, freeing capacity and capital while keeping BRIX the one-stop destination.

What drives breakeven?

GMV mix, delivery fee coverage, rent from concessions, and inventory turns; scenario selector shows timing.

What’s the inventory discipline policy?

Brand redundancy by design, strict turns targets, and category-by-category dealership vs concession decisions.
